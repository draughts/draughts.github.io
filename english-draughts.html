Draughts

The English draughts are an abstract combinatorial strategy game of the family of the Checkers. English draughts are played mainly in English-speaking countries and Italy.

English draughts Rules

  • Size of the tray: 64 boxes (8×8) ;
  • Number of pieces: 24 (2×12) ;
  • The orientation of the plateau: Large diagonal from left to right ;
  • Used boxes: Black boxes ;
  • The draughts player with the initiative: Whites ;
  • Authorized take of pieces: Front diagonals only ;
  • Take-up constraint: Optional majority take-up, but any round must go to the end ;
  • The qualitative catch: Optional ;
  • Lady: Moves only one space ;
  • Removing the pawns taken: After the roundup ;
  • Promotion by the way: Prohibited.

Variants related to openness

Two variants of the English draughts are practiced in competition:

  • the variant GAYP ( Go as you please ): no constraint at the beginning of the draughts game;
  • the 3-shot variant: the first three moves are randomly drawn from among a set of sequences considered fair, the players play two English draughts games by exchanging the sides.
